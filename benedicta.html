What My Heart Has Been Trying to Say

Benedicta — even your name sounds like a blessing.
Like something the universe whispered into existence
just to give beauty a name it could finally wear.

✦

I have watched the way you move through the world —
how your presence doesn't just fill a room,
it quietly rewrites it.
And I have been undone by it, every single time.

I am Joseph Nutsa, and I come to you
not with grand theatrics or borrowed words —
but with the most honest thing I have:
a heart that has chosen you, quietly and completely.

I am in love with you.

In love with the way you think.
In love with what you don't say out loud.
In love with the version of myself
that only seems to exist when you're near.

I have spent time trying to talk myself out of this feeling —
and every time I failed, beautifully,
because some things are bigger than logic.
You are one of those things.

If you let me,
I will spend every ordinary Tuesday making you feel extraordinary.
I will be patient in the hard seasons.
I will celebrate every small version of you — not just the bright ones.
I will be someone you can trust with the parts of yourself
you haven't shown the world yet.

✦

"You are the poem I never knew I was writing.
The chapter I didn't plan but can't imagine skipping.
The answer to a question my heart had been asking
in the language of longing — long before I found you."

✦

— Joseph Nutsa
